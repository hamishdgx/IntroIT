Project Idea

I have had quite a lot of trouble brainstorming ideas for this task, after talks with the people around me and what they think is needed in this world of IT I will be creating an application for finding herbs in your area. This will be a website that is mobile friendly that contains community information on where there is herbs planted in your area that you can take and propagate to grow your own herbs, this website will also give descriptions of the herbs with photos and use-cases. I will be building this so that public people can add information to it to help others.

This idea sprung to mind after talking to my partner who is in University for biology, specifically conservation and climate change. She has an interest to plants and knows all the plants in our area. I thought that a map of plants around the area would be interesting and fun, although I thought that this was not feasible and quite a big task. With "Herb and Spice Processing industries revenue anticipated to grow in the next 5 years" (ibisworld.com, 2021) it is safe to assume that more people are trying exotic dishes in their cooking and therefor using more herbs, herb gardens are an easy and sustainable way to reduce processing and carbon emissions while helping out a neighbourhood; but many do not know that they exist or where to find specific herbs which is why a website will help the local community save money and time.

By creating a website where you can input your postcode or alternatively use your GPS with the google maps API, it will show you both in list form and on the map where to find all the herbs in your area. Of course this is a lot of data to input as a developer and I cannot go around all of Melbourne/Australia finding every herb garden, therefor there will be an option to add herb gardens that consumers have found/grown themselves; this adds in a community aspect to the website and will increase its accuracy with the option for consumers to "like" the location to confirm its existence and how well maintained the garden is.

Alongside this map will be a list of the sightings/herbs in the area which will update based on the map view, this will include information such as how far away it is, its actual location/address, the name of the herb and how many people have liked and confirmed its existence. The list view will also allow you go to the index of herbs, which will be a list of all the herbs in the area detailing specifics such as names, descriptions, a photo for some, and uses in both foods and potential medicine. The index of herb will not be accessible to edit from the pubic in order to ensure reliable information. The website will be hosted in only English at the start as Australia mainly speaks English and as a single developer, I do not have the recourses to accommodate much accessibility; although in the future potential accessibility changes can be made.

The website's design will very much be comforting and inviting, as it is not a very professional application and is much more for hobbyist cooks and average people the design will be suited to the demographic of being very safe with non-intensive colours mainly focusing on pastels. The user interface will be very standard for a lot of glossary/index websites, but with the main focus of the website being the map. Due to this being a small project bugs are bound to appear even post development, so having a page where people can submit bug finds with details on the bug and how they created it will be very useful.

The input for the map and list will be on a separate page accessible from the header where a user can input the herb from a list that is searchable for the herb they found, small description of the area, an optional photo and the location of the herb which could be a direct address or pin on a map. The reason for the pin on the map option is because many gardens are off streets and will not have specific address's or at least not easily found address's. I believe that the user input is extremely important for a website such as this, not only for its usability but also to create a sense of community in the website.

The technologies required for this website are quite simple but important, it will need a basic webserver capable of running the google maps API in specific the MAPS JavaScript API as it will need custom content (developers.google.com) this means that my webserver will also be needing to run basic JavaScript. Due to there being custom content and information stored from user input, a simple database will need to be used; something my MySQL or SQLite will be sufficient as to not overload the cloud instance used. For the webhosting, a physical server is expensive these days and something simple like a small website is not necessary, a cloud instance from a provider such as AWS EC2 or Digital Ocean droplets will be needed to host both the SQL server and the web server.

In regards to skills needed to create this application, a decent knowledge of both HTML and CSS is necessary to create the visuals and functionality of the site, and a simple knowledge of JavaScript will be needed to implement the MAPS API. Alongside this a decent knowledge of how to use both AWS or other cloud providers including implementing databases into an instance and both pulling and pushing to said database. I do not believe any special hardware is necessary although the skills and software are all available to be used easily, there is plenty of HTML and CSS training courses online on top of simple AWS ones.

If the project is successful I believe it will actually help people, even if it is just removing a minor inconvenience of having to find/buy herbs. Aswell as removing this inconvenience, I believe websites such as these create a sense of community which strengthens the area that many live in and provides a potential form of communication that would normally not be there. With this website it will be easy to find free and sustainable herbs to use when necessary. And through this development I believe that not only will people's lives be impacted but it will reduce the unnecessary waste of plastics being put on herbs in supermarkets as well as the carbon produced from the processing of these herbs; Aswell as making everyone's cooking just a little better.
